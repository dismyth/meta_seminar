Fall 2022 Topic: Categories and Types

Type theory is an extension of first-order logic in which terms range not only over objects in a domain of discourse, but also over sets, relations, and functions on these objects. New syntactic objects called types are used to organize the terms, ensuring that they are combined and invoked in logically meaningful ways.
Category theory can be thought as an abstract algebraic formulation of set theory in which the notion of a map between sets is treated as primary rather than the usual element membership relation. It has proved to be an enormously useful tool for studying ``structure" across a broad range of disciplines. In particular, category theory has entered mathematical logic with the realization that suitably structured categories can be used as models of various type theories. We will have several introductory lectures on category theory and type theory with a eye toward:
  • In what sense (if any) is category theory an alternative to set theory as a foundation for mathematics?
  • How can category theory help computers prove theorems?
  • Is category theory useful for formal semantics or metaphysics?

    About the Seminar

    The Metaseminar is interested in mathematical logic, as well as formal methods more generally across a broad range of disciplines, e.g. mathematics, philosophy, linguistics, economics, and music theory. We are interested not only in mathematical questions about these formal methods (i.e. how they work), but also philosophical questions (such as their ultimate aims and limitations), and historical/sociological questions (i.e. why certain systems gain prestige and become standard)
    The Metaseminar is part working group, part seminar. We’re a working group in the sense that, each semester, we pick a book/topic and try to make some concerted progress towards understanding it. We’re a seminar in that we enjoy having guest speakers and one-off lectures on a wide variety of topics. Undergraduate students, graduate students, and faculty from all disciplines are welcome to attend.
