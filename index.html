Description

Category Theory can be thought of as a set of abstract algebraic methods for studying “structure” across a broad range of problems and disciplines. Since being developed in the 1940s, it has found applications to programming languages, mathematical logic, music theory, and physics. We will have several lectures on Category Theory, with an eye to:
  • In what sense (if any) is category theory an alternative to set theory as a foundation for mathematics?
  • How can category theory help computers prove theorems?
  • Is category theory useful for formal semantics or formal epistemology?

    About the Seminar

    The Metaseminar is interested in mathematical logic, as well as formal methods more generally across a broad range of disciplines, e.g. mathematics, philosophy, linguistics, economics, and music theory. We are interested not only in mathematical questions about these formal methods (i.e. how they work), philosophical questions (such as their ultimate aims and limitations), and historical/sociological questions (i.e. why certain systems gain prestige and become standard)
    The Metaseminar is part working group, part seminar. We’re a working group in the sense that, each semester, we pick a book/topic and try to make some concerted progress towards understanding it. We’re a seminar in that we enjoy having guest speakers and one-off lectures on a wide variety of topics.
